# Welcome Address to Computing Initiates

## Welcome

To all of you joining us, welcome.

We communicate with computers, and they with us, on a daily basis. They idle within reach of our bodies. They respond to our voice, and to our touch. We have sent them into the atmosphere, to near celestial bodies, and even beyond the confines of our solar system.

They form a vast network of tools, a fraction of which could not be understood by any single person in many lifetimes.

In this course we will touch on the foundational principles of modern computing.

## Importance of Computing

As our species rapidly co-evolves with the technologies that we've created, it's vitally important to have an understanding of how they operate, and the context in which they were created.

Computers form the backbone of our recent history. Knowledge of the context in which our technology was created positions us to better evaluate the context in which it's currently being applied. It provides lessons so that we don't have to start from scratch or repeat past mistakes.

Computers offer the best hope to solving many modern pressing problems. Knowledge of their implementation helps us to draw upon creative patterns when forming solutions.

Computers have changed the organ most responsible for shaping our world - the human brain. Emotional health of technology users has changed as a result of its use. We have seen cognitive changes among "digital natives" and heavy tech users that we don't yet fully understand. We can never be "done with" studying computing technology as it relates to us, the users.

## Possibilities and Dangers

Digital computers have driven extraordinary transition at a macro human level, accelarating us rapidly forward into the Information Age. The possibility of an egalitarian distribution of information, with the sum of human knowledge available to any man or woman, is now closer than at any other time. With such freedom of information comes a democracy of ideas. Poor decisions that result from a small and biased set of facts can give way to those that are rounded and holistic.

With our new power, however, comes danger.

If we fail to examine the value that a given technology provides us we run the risk of using it habitually, without seeing that there is a choice to set it aside or change it. We must reflect upon the behavioral changes that a technology has in our personal lives, and the lives of those around us. We must resist the temptation to chase instant gratification and fleeting novelty, and instead focus on the long term benefits that a technology can provide.

If we fail to draw new minds into the field of computing we may be left with too few who understand it. In such a case we would be left with a handful of powerful decision makers. Whether well or ill intentioned, the actions of these individuals, corporations, or agencies, would weigh heavily on society. We must take seriously the study of computing in order to avoid an oligarchy of tech experts. Community feedback is required to keep societal values in balance.

If we fail to scrutinize those who are making technological decisions, those decisions may fall into the hands of people who are overly motivated by their own fears. We would run the risk of either innovation collapse (driven by those who view technology as dark magic, and would confine its development) or of an assault on competing views (driven by those who are afraid of the "other", and would use technology as a weapon). We must boldly advocate for open and transparent tech policy, balancing personal incentives with social responsibility.

## Hope for the Future

I have continued to see in the generations coming out of higher education brilliant, inspiring, and wonderful things. The personal, societal, and existential challenges that lie ahead are daunting, but I remain optimistic in view of this.

It's very possible that in the near future the Information Age may give birth to something new and different. By exploring what goes into computing you've positioned yourself to shape this world from the standpoint of an insider.

The collective project of humanity - that of preserving our strange and precarious position in the universe - depends upon people who are willing to push through their own personal discomfort. Whether or not you choose to make a career in computing, I hope that this course will position you to better navigate our world as you enact thoughtful, deliberate, and positive change.

## Closing

Most of you are at a volatile transition point in your lives. There are things that you'll have to leave behind in order to explore the possibilities that lie ahead.

There is no growth without risk. The most successful people are usually those who have failed the most.

You're capable of more than you think.
