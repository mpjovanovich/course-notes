# Welcome Address to Computing Initiates

## Welcome

To all of you joining us, welcome.

We communicate with computers, and they with us, on a daily basis. They respond to our voice, and to our touch. They idle within reach of our bodies, and they signal to us from beyond the confines of our solar system.

They form a vast network of tools, a fraction of which could not be understood by any single person in many lifetimes.

In this course we will touch on the foundational principles of modern computing.

## Importance of Computing

As our species rapidly co-evolves with the technologies that we've created, it's vitally important to maintain an understanding of how they operate, and where they came from.

Computers form the backbone of our recent history. Knowledge of the context in which our technology was created positions us to better evaluate the context in which it's currently being applied. It provides lessons so that we don't have to start from scratch or repeat past mistakes.

Computers offer the best hope to solving many modern pressing problems. Knowledge of their implementation helps us to draw upon creative patterns when forming solutions.

Computers have changed the one organ most responsible for shaping our world - the human brain. Emotional health of technology users has changed as a result of its use. We have seen cognitive changes among "digital natives" and heavy tech users that we don't yet fully understand. We can never be "done with" studying computing technology as it relates to us, the users.

## Possibilities and Dangers

Digital computers have driven extraordinary transition at a macro human level, accelarating us rapidly forward into the Information Age. The possibility of an egalitarian distribution of information, with the sum of human knowledge available to any man or woman, is now closer than at any other time. With such freedom of information comes a democracy of ideas. Poor decisions that result from a small and biased set of facts can give way to those that are rounded and holistic.

With our new power, however, comes danger.

If we fail to examine the value that a given technology provides us we run the risk of using it habitually, without seeing that there is a choice to set it aside or change it. We must focus on the long term benefits that a technology can provide, and continually assess its value.

If we fail to draw new minds into the field of computing we may be left with a handful of powerful tech decision makers whose decisions have undue consequences. We must maintain a diverse and balanced computing community so that the failure of any one tech leader, corporation, or government does not have devastating effects.

If we fail to scrutinize those who are making tech decisions, we may allow decision makers to stifle innovation or suppress competing voices. We must boldly advocate for open and transparent tech policies.

## Hope for the Future

I have continued to see in the generations coming out of higher education brilliant, inspiring, and wonderful things. The personal, societal, and existential challenges that lie ahead are daunting, but I remain optimistic in view of this.

It's very possible that in the near future the Information Age may give way to something new and different. By exploring what goes into computing you've positioned yourself to shape this world from the standpoint of an insider.

The collective project of humanity - that of preserving our strange and precarious position in the universe - depends upon people who are willing to push through their own personal discomfort. Whether or not you choose to make a career in computing, I hope that this course will position you to better navigate our world as you enact thoughtful, deliberate, and positive change.

## Closing

Most of you are at a volatile transition point in your lives. There are things that you'll have to leave behind in order to make the most of the possibilities that lie ahead.

There is no growth without risk. Please don't be afraid to fail, as each failure is also a lesson. The smartest and most successful people have failed much more often than those who never tried.

In summary: _If you start pushing your boundaries I'm certain that you'll find that you're capable of more than you think._
